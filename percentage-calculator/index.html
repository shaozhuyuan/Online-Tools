Understanding Percentages: Equations, formulas & adjustments

A percentage is a number or ratio expressed as a fraction of 100. It is often denoted using the percent sign "%". Percentages are dimensionless numbers and have no associated unit of measurement.

General Percentage Formula:

Percentage = (Part / Whole) × 100

Percentage Increase/Decrease Formula:

To calculate the percentage difference between two numbers $V_1$ (original) and $V_2$ (new):

Percentage Change = ((V₂ - V₁) / |V₁|) × 100

If the result is positive, it represents an increase. If negative, it represents a decrease.

Real-World Applications

  • Financial Margins: Markup and gross profit margins are calculated as percentages of cost and revenue.
  • Sales & Discounts: "Save 25% today!" requires calculating 25% of the original price and subtracting it.
  • Interest Rates & Inflation: Annual Percentage Rates (APR) and inflation indexes represent yearly compounded growths.
  • School Grades: Test scores are calculated as the percentage of correct answers out of the total questions.
